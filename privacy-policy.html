Privacy Policy.

How we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our Site.

twRty is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, use our software-as-a-service (SaaS) products, mobile applications, or any other media or service related or connected thereto (collectively, the "Site" and "Services"). Please read this Privacy Policy carefully.

If you do not agree with the terms of this Privacy Policy, please do not access the Site or use our Services.

We reserve the right to make changes to this Privacy Policy at any time and for any reason. We will alert you about any changes by updating the "Last Updated" date of this Privacy Policy. You are encouraged to periodically review this Privacy Policy to stay informed of updates. Continued use of the Site or Services after the revised Privacy Policy is posted constitutes acceptance of the changes.

Data Controller

The data controller responsible for personal information collected through this Site and our Services is twRty Software Services.

For privacy questions or to exercise your rights, contact:

Email: support@twrty.org
Correspondence address: Virtual Office, Ahmedabad, Gujarat, India

Information We Collect

We may collect the following categories of personal information:

Information you provide directly: name, email address, phone number, account credentials, profile photo, billing details, and any message or content you submit through forms or our Services.

Subscription and payment information: subscription identifier, plan, currency (USD or INR depending on region), payment status, and billing period. Card and bank-account details are entered directly into our payment processor's secure environment and are not stored on our servers.

Content you create: blog ideas, drafts, images, and other material you generate using our Services.

Technical information: IP address, browser type, device type and identifier, operating system, language, time zone, referring URL, and usage data such as pages viewed and features used.

Information from third-party logins: if you sign in with Google or another identity provider, we receive the basic profile information that provider shares (typically name, email, and avatar URL).

How We Use Your Information

We use personal information to: (a) operate, maintain, and provide the Site and Services; (b) create and manage your account; (c) process subscriptions, payments, and refunds; (d) respond to your inquiries and provide customer support; (e) send transactional notifications, security alerts, and service updates; (f) improve and personalize our Services; (g) detect, prevent, and address fraud, abuse, and security incidents; (h) comply with legal obligations.

Lawful Basis for Processing

Where the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), the UK GDPR, or equivalent laws apply, we process personal information on the following lawful bases:

Consent — when you opt in to marketing communications, non-essential cookies, or share information voluntarily.

Performance of a contract — when processing is necessary to provide a Service you have requested or paid for, including subscription management.

Legitimate interests — to operate, secure, analyse, and improve our Services, where these interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms.

Legal obligation — when required to comply with applicable law, court orders, or regulatory requests.

How We Protect Your Information

We implement appropriate technical and organisational measures — including encryption in transit (TLS), access controls, principle-of-least-privilege roles, audit logging, and regular security reviews — to safeguard personal data against unauthorised access, disclosure, alteration, and destruction. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to apply commercially reasonable safeguards.

Subscriptions and Payments

When you subscribe to our paid Services, payments are processed by a trusted third-party payment processor. Card details, UPI credentials, and bank-account information are entered directly into the processor's secure checkout and are never stored on our servers. We retain only the minimum information needed to manage your subscription, such as the subscription identifier, plan, currency, payment status, and billing period.

Disclosure of Your Information

We do not sell or rent personal information. We may share personal information with trusted categories of service providers who help us operate the Site and Services and are contractually bound to safeguard it and use it only as instructed by us. These categories include:

Hosting, database, and authentication providers; payment processors; content-delivery and cloud providers; email and notification services; and analytics tools (only when you have consented to non-essential cookies).

We may also disclose personal information when required by law, to enforce our terms, or to protect the rights, safety, or property of twRty, our users, or others.

Your Rights

Under the GDPR, the UK GDPR, and other applicable data protection laws, you have the following rights:

Right of access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you.
Right to rectification — request correction of inaccurate or incomplete information.
Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten") — request deletion of your data, subject to legal exceptions.
Right to restriction of processing — request that we limit processing under certain conditions.
Right to data portability — receive your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
Right to object — object to processing based on legitimate interests or to direct marketing.
Right to withdraw consent — withdraw consent at any time, without affecting the lawfulness of prior processing.
Right to lodge a complaint — file a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority (in the EU, this is the authority of your country of residence).

To exercise any of these rights, contact support@twrty.org. We will respond within 30 days and may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

Data Retention

We retain personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy:

Account information — retained while your account is active. After account deletion, residual copies may persist in routine backups for up to 90 days, after which they are securely overwritten.
Subscription and payment records — retained for up to 7 years to meet financial-record and tax obligations.
Support correspondence — retained for up to 2 years from the last interaction.
Analytics data — retained in aggregated, anonymised form.

International Data Transfers

Personal information may be transferred to, stored in, and processed in countries outside your jurisdiction, including India and other regions where our service providers operate. Where data is transferred from the European Economic Area (EEA), the United Kingdom, or Switzerland to a country not deemed adequate by the relevant authority, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) approved under Article 46 of the GDPR, together with supplementary technical and organisational measures where required.

California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act, as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act, provides you additional rights, including the right to know what categories of personal information we collect, the right to request deletion, the right to correct inaccurate information, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising these rights.

We do not sell or share personal information as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA. To exercise your California rights, contact support@twrty.org.

Use of AI and Automated Technologies

twRty may use artificial intelligence (AI), automation, or data-driven technologies within its software solutions and internal processes to enhance functionality, performance, and user experience. These technologies are designed to operate responsibly and are subject to appropriate human oversight. We do not use personal data for solely automated decision-making that produces legal or similarly significant effects without your consent.

Data Security and Breach Response

In the event of a data breach that may compromise your personal information, we will take prompt action to investigate, mitigate potential harm, and notify affected users and relevant supervisory authorities as required by applicable laws (including, where applicable, within 72 hours of becoming aware of a notifiable breach under the GDPR).

Children's Privacy

Our Site and Services are not intended for children under the age of 13 (or 16 in the European Economic Area). We do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If we become aware that such information has been collected, we will take steps to delete it promptly.

Third-Party Links

Our Site and Services may include links to third-party products or services. These third parties have separate and independent privacy policies. We have no responsibility or liability for the content, practices, or activities of these linked sites or services.

Online Privacy Policy Only

This online privacy policy applies only to information collected through our Site and Services and not to information collected offline.

Cookies Policy

This website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to make the Site work, analyse traffic, and (with your consent) support marketing efforts. We use a granular consent banner so that only essential cookies are set by default and analytics or marketing cookies load only after you opt in.

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking "Cookie preferences" in the site footer.

What are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They help the site function, remember your preferences, and measure performance.

Types of Cookies We May Use

Essential cookies (always on) — necessary for the Site to function, including security, navigation, and remembering your cookie consent choice.

Analytics cookies (opt-in) — help us understand how visitors interact with the Site by collecting aggregated, anonymised data.

Marketing cookies (opt-in) — used to measure the effectiveness of marketing campaigns and, where applicable, deliver relevant advertising.

Your Consent

By using our Site, you consent to our Privacy Policy. By choosing your cookie preferences in the consent banner, you control which non-essential cookies are set on your device.

Changes to Our Privacy Policy

If we change this Privacy Policy, we will post the updated version on this page and revise the "Last Updated" date at the top.
